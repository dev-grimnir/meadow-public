Plan view from above — north is up, the road edge is at the left and the slope rises to the toe (top of the slope) at the right. The fall line runs left–right and is stopped at each 12″ grass contour band (the green columns). Planting beds are the columns between the bands, sized by measured bed area and colored by flower color, with species stacked north‑to‑south; hover or tap for the species. Source: gardenmap.md.

  • Green columns = 12″ grass contour bands (road, mid, toe) — a staple-anchored erosion scaffold that runs the full slope length, left standing until the meadow's roots take over, then removed.
  • Beds are seeded short-in-front (road side, left) to tall-in-back (toe side, right). Hatched panel = juglone zone around the black-walnut corner; every species there is juglone-tolerant. Dashed panel = butterfly territory (both monarch hosts + the black-swallowtail host).
